<p><ul><li>Dehydrated Food</li><li>Freeze-dried Food</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The long-term food storage market primarily includes dehydrated and freeze-dried food types. Dehydrated food is produced by removing moisture to inhibit microbial growth, making it lightweight and shelf-stable. However, it retains fewer nutrients and flavor. Freeze-dried food, on the other hand, involves freezing the food and then removing moisture through sublimation, preserving its nutrients, texture, and taste effectively. Both methods cater to consumers seeking convenient, long-lasting food solutions for emergencies, camping, or outdoor adventures.</p></p>
