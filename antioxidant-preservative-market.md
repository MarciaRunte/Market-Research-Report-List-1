<p><p>The antioxidant preservative market includes several key types, each serving to extend the shelf life of products. Ascorbic acid, a vitamin C derivative, prevents oxidation in food and beverages. Erythorbic acid, a stereoisomer of ascorbic acid, also inhibits spoilage. Propyl gallate is commonly used in fats and oils to prevent rancidity. Tocopherols, including vitamin E compounds, act as natural antioxidants, protecting against oxidative damage. Together, these preservatives enhance product stability and safety in various industries.</p></p>
