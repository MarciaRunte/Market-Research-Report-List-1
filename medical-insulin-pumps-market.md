<p><p>The medical insulin pumps market consists of two main types: patch pumps and traditional insulin pumps. Patch pumps are discrete, adhesive devices that deliver insulin and can be worn directly on the skin, offering convenience and flexibility. Traditional insulin pumps are larger, with a reservoir, tubing, and a control unit, providing customizable insulin delivery. Both types aim to improve diabetes management by offering users greater control over their insulin dosing, thus enhancing their quality of life and ensuring better glycemic control.</p></p>
