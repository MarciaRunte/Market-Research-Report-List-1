<p><p>Titanate ceramics are categorized into two primary market types: oxide and non-oxide. Oxide titanates, primarily composed of titanium dioxide and various metal oxides, exhibit excellent thermal stability, mechanical strength, and dielectric properties, making them suitable for electronic applications and insulators. In contrast, non-oxide titanates typically include compounds like titanium carbide or titanate composites, offering distinct advantages in high-temperature applications due to their superior toughness and resistance to oxidation. Both types play vital roles in diverse industrial applications.</p></p>
