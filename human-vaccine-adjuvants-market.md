<p><p>The human vaccine adjuvants market is categorized based on administration routes, including oral, subcutaneous, intramuscular, and others. Oral adjuvants are designed for vaccine delivery through the digestive system, potentially enhancing mucosal immunity. Subcutaneous adjuvants are administered beneath the skin, stimulating localized immune responses. Intramuscular adjuvants are injected into muscle tissue, promoting robust systemic immunity. Other delivery methods may encompass intranasal or transdermal routes, each aiming to improve vaccine efficacy and immune response while addressing specific patient needs.</p></p>
