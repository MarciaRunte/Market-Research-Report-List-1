<p><p>The interbody spacers market includes two primary types: polyetheretherketone (PEEK) and titanium. PEEK spacers are favored for their biocompatibility, radiolucency, and flexibility, allowing for better imaging and adaptability to spinal motion. Titanium spacers, on the other hand, offer superior strength, osseointegration, and durability, making them ideal for high-load applications. Both materials play a critical role in spinal fusion surgeries, addressing various patient needs while enhancing overall surgical outcomes and recovery rates.</p></p>
