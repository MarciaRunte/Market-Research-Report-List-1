<p><p>The automotive gear lubrication market features two primary types: mineral lubricants and synthetic lubricants. Mineral lubricants are derived from refining crude oil and provide adequate protection and performance for conventional vehicles, often at a lower cost. Synthetic lubricants, on the other hand, are chemically engineered for enhanced performance, offering superior protection against wear, temperature resistance, and longer fluid life. They are ideal for high-performance and specialized vehicles, meeting advanced specifications and improving overall efficiency and longevity of gear systems.</p></p>
