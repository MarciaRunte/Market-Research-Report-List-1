<p><p>Learner Driver Insurance markets can be categorized into two main types: Own Car and Someone Else's Car. The Own Car market targets learners who are practicing in their own vehicles, allowing coverage for personal liability and potential damages. Conversely, the Someone Else's Car market caters to learners using a friend or family member’s vehicle, often requiring the car owner's existing policy to extend coverage. Both markets aim to provide financial protection during the learning process, addressing the risks associated with novice drivers.</p></p>
