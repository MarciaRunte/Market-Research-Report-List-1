<p><p>Electrostatic Chucks (ESCs) are critical components in semiconductor manufacturing, used to hold and stabilize wafers during processing. Two primary types are the Coulomb Type, which relies on electrostatic forces generated by an applied voltage, and the Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type, which enhances wafer stability by employing a combination of electrostatic and dielectric effects. Both types ensure precise alignment and contact, improving process efficiency and yield. The choice between them depends on specific application requirements and performance needs in the semiconductor market.</p></p>
