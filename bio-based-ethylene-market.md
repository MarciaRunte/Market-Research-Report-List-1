Starch, sourced from corn, potatoes, and other grains, undergoes hydrolysis to produce sugars for ethylene production. Lignocellulosic biomass, including agricultural residues and wood, offers a sustainable alternative; it is processed through various methods to release fermentable sugars, promoting a circular economy in bio-based ethylene production.</p></p>
