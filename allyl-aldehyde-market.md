<p><p>Allyl aldehyde, also known as acrolein, is a highly reactive unsaturated aldehyde with the chemical formula C3H4O. It is a colorless or yellowish liquid with a pungent, acrid odor and is primarily used as an intermediate in the production of chemicals such as methionine, acrylic acid, and pharmaceuticals. It is also employed in water treatment, oilfield chemicals, and as a biocide.
