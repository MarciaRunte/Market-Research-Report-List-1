<p><p>Radiation shielding systems are designed to protect against harmful radiation exposure. The market is segmented based on the type of radiation: **Indirectly Ionizing Radiation** (e.g., X-rays, gamma rays) requires dense materials like lead or concrete to absorb energy and prevent penetration. **Directly Ionizing Radiation** (e.g., alpha, beta particles) involves charged particles that lose energy through collisions, requiring lighter materials like plastic or aluminum. Both systems aim to minimize health risks by blocking or reducing radiation exposure in medical, nuclear, and industrial applications.</p></p>
